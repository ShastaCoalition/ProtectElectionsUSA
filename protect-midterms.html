Founded on the 10th Amendment

Powers not delegated to the federal government are reserved to the States — and the people. States have the constitutional authority and responsibility to protect elections from federal interference.

🛡

We Are Not Powerless — And Here’s Why

State criminal law is beyond the reach of federal pardons. A President cannot pardon violations of state statutes. That means state-level legislation criminalizing election interference, illegal voter purges, and unauthorized surrender of voter data creates accountability that no federal administration can erase. We have the power right now — at the state level — to pass laws that will hold election saboteurs accountable, no matter who sits in the White House.

⏰ The Clock Is Running Out — Act Before May

California’s legislative window closes mid-September — but the real power window is right now through May. After that, bills get harder to pass and changes may not take effect before November.

NOW
🔥 You Are Here — March
Highest Influence Window — Act Immediately

Bills are still being introduced. Language can still be shaped. Grassroots pressure is most effective right now. This is the moment your calls matter most.

APR
🔴 Still Strong — April
Policy Committee Decisions

Bills are in committee review. Public testimony and constituent pressure can still kill bad bills and advance good ones. Don’t let up.

MAY
⚠️ Narrowing — May–June
Fiscal Review & House-of-Origin Deadline

Bills must pass their first chamber or they die. Budget provisions must move through the June process. Urgency legislation can still take effect immediately if passed now.

SEPT
🔒 Final Deadline — Mid-September
Legislature Stops. Campaign Season Begins.

After mid-September, no new laws pass. What isn’t done by then won’t protect the November election. There is no second chance.

👉 Bottom line: We have approximately 6–8 weeks of peak legislative influence remaining. Every week of delay is a week of protection lost. Call your Assembly Member and State Senator this week.

⚖ Three Essential State Legal Actions
Demand your state legislators author and pass these protections before the midterms.
Ban Now 🚫

Ban All Third-Party Voter Challenges

End “vigilante” purge lists and digital spreadsheets used by outside groups to disenfranchise legitimate voters. No private group should have the power to remove citizens from the rolls.

Protect Now 🛡

Prohibit Voter & Election Worker Intimidation

Establish broad prohibitions backed by criminal prosecution and civil penalties. Poll workers and voters must be safe from harassment, threats, and coercion — period.

Require Now 🏛

Mandate Ministerial (Clerical) Certification

Remove official discretion from election certification. Make it a simple, non-discretionary clerical requirement — officials must certify valid results, no exceptions, no delays.

🎯 Target All Six State Power Centers
Each official has specific powers to protect elections right now — most without waiting for new legislation. Call all six — coordinate with others to do the same week.
🏙

Governor

  • Issue executive orders barring state agencies and county election offices from assisting voter purges — no legislation needed
  • Make it a state felony to surrender voter data to the DOJ or any federal agency without a court order
  • Deploy state resources to protect polling sites & commit to certifying all valid results
  • Sign any election protection bills immediately upon passage
⚖

Attorney General

  • Investigate and prosecute now — any county Registrar who has provided voter data to the DOJ without a court order
  • Sue immediately to block illegal third-party voter purge lists
  • Issue legal opinions affirming ministerial certification is non-discretionary
  • Create a dedicated election protection task force
💰

State Treasurer

  • Refuse to disburse state funds to counties conducting illegal voter purges
  • Withhold payment to vendors facilitating voter suppression tools
  • Publicly report any federal funding threats to election administration
📈

State Controller

  • Audit election administration spending for irregularities
  • Flag any unauthorized federal access to state election funds
  • Ensure election offices are fully funded through the midterms
🏛

State Assembly Member

  • Champion and introduce legislation banning third-party voter challenges
  • Champion mandatory ministerial certification legislation
  • Request Assembly hearings on county Registrars surrendering voter data to federal agencies
  • Champion legislation criminalizing unauthorized voter data transfers
🏛

State Senator

  • Champion and carry election protection bills through the Senate with urgency designation
  • Request Senate hearings on election interference by local officials
  • Champion legislation making voter data transfers to federal agencies a felony
  • Ensure bills reach floor vote before September deadline

📳 What to Say When You Call
Keep it under 60 seconds. Say your name, your city, and one clear ask. Here are four ready-to-use scripts.
🏙 For the Governor
“Hi, my name is [NAME] and I’m a constituent in [CITY]. I’m calling to urge Governor Newsom to immediately issue an executive order barring all state agencies and county election offices from surrendering voter data to the DOJ or any federal agency without a court order. County Registrars are already doing this — we need action now, before the midterms.”
⚖ For the Attorney General
“Hi, my name is [NAME] from [CITY]. I’m calling to urge AG Bonta to investigate and prosecute any county Registrar who has provided voter data to the DOJ without a court order, and to create a dedicated election protection task force immediately. This is happening right now and we need him to act.”
💰 For the Treasurer & 📈 Controller
“Hi, my name is [NAME] from [CITY]. I’m calling to urge [OFFICIAL] to use their financial oversight authority to refuse disbursement of state funds to any county cooperating with illegal voter purges or unauthorized federal requests for voter data. Please protect California’s elections now.”
🏛 For Your Assembly Member or State Senator
“Hi, my name is [NAME] and I’m a constituent in [CITY]. I’m calling to urge [LEGISLATOR] to champion and introduce legislation banning third-party voter challenges, prohibiting voter intimidation, and mandating ministerial certification of election results. These bills need urgency designation to take effect before November. Will [LEGISLATOR] commit to championing this legislation?”
💡 Pro tip: Don’t read it word for word — just use it as a guide. A natural, personal call is always more powerful than a scripted one. Staff will ask your ZIP CODE — have it ready.
